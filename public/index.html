Position sizing — This is one of the most important skills in trading. It means deciding how much of your account to risk on any single trade. The AI recommends a percentage based on how confident it is in the signal.

HIGH confidence → Risk up to 10% of balance. When many indicators agree strongly, you can put a bit more in.

MEDIUM confidence → Risk 3–5%. Decent setup but not everything is lined up perfectly.

LOW confidence → Risk only 2%. The indicators are mixed. This is a risky trade — minimal exposure only.

Why not risk it all? — Even high-confidence trades fail. If you risk 100% and lose, you're done. By keeping each trade small, you can survive losing streaks and still reach the goal. Professionals call this "capital preservation."

The math of the challenge — To go from $10 to $10,000 you need to 1000x your money. That sounds impossible but with consistent 20–30% wins per trade and compounding, it becomes achievable over time with discipline.

⚠️ RULES — DO NOT BREAK:
Why exits matter more than entries — Most beginners focus on WHEN to get in. But the difference between profitable traders and losing ones is knowing WHEN TO GET OUT. Hold too long = watch profits disappear. Exit too early = miss gains. The AI gives you specific conditions so you don't have to guess.

Same-day trading rule — For day trades, always exit before 3:45 PM ET. Options lose value fast near close and near expiration. Don't let a winning trade turn into a loser because you held too long.

The 50% rule — If your option doubles in value (up 100%), consider selling half and letting the rest run. This locks in profit while keeping upside open.
TRADE PLAN
CALL — Betting the stock goes UP. Profit if it rises above strike before expiration.

PUT — Betting the stock goes DOWN. Profit if it falls below strike before expiration.

Strike Price — The price the stock needs to reach for your option to be profitable at expiration.

Expiration — The deadline. For day trades: today or tomorrow (0DTE/1DTE). Cheap but fast-moving.
