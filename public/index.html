Why same-day trading needs strict exits — Options lose value every single day just from time passing (called "theta decay"). If you hold past market close or into expiration day, your option can lose most of its value even if TSLA moves in your direction. Getting out before 3:45 PM ET is critical for day trades.

The 5 sell signals — These are the specific conditions that tell you the trade is turning against you or has reached its goal. You don't need ALL of them to happen — just ONE is enough reason to exit.

Profit taking — On a day trade, locking in 30–50% gain is often smarter than waiting for 100%. Options can swing back fast. A bird in hand is worth two in the bush.

Stop loss discipline — This is the #1 skill in trading. The moment your stop loss is hit, sell. No second-guessing. No "it'll come back." Protecting your capital is more important than any single trade.

12 LIVE TECHNICAL INDICATORS
RSI — Above 70 = overbought. Below 30 = oversold. For day trading, RSI moving fast is more important than the exact number.

MACD — Bullish Cross = buy signal. Bearish Cross = sell signal. Watch for crossovers during the day.

EMA 20/50/200 — Short-term trend (20), medium (50), long-term (200). Price above all = strong uptrend.

Stochastic & Williams %R — Fast momentum indicators. Great for day trading entries and exits.

OBV — Is volume confirming the price move? Rising OBV with rising price = strong. Divergence = warning.

ATR — Daily range. Higher ATR = more volatile, bigger moves possible in your direction (or against you).
