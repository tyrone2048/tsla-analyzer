YOUR $10 TRADE PLAN
CALL Option — Bet that TSLA goes UP. You profit if TSLA rises above your strike price before expiration.

PUT Option — Bet that TSLA goes DOWN. You profit if TSLA falls below your strike price before expiration.

Strike Price — The target price you're betting on. The stock needs to move past this in your favor for the option to be profitable at expiration.

Expiration — Your deadline. After this the option has no value if it hasn't moved your way. Longer = more time but more expensive.

12 LIVE TECHNICAL INDICATORS
RSI (14) — 0–100. Above 70 = overbought (may fall). Below 30 = oversold (may rise). 40–60 = neutral.

MACD — Momentum indicator. Positive & rising = bullish. Negative & falling = bearish. Crossovers signal trend changes.

EMA 20/50/200 — Moving averages. Price above all three = strong uptrend. Below all three = strong downtrend. The 200 EMA is the most important long-term trend line.

Bollinger Bands — Near upper band = potentially overbought. Near lower band = potentially oversold. Price breaking out of bands = strong momentum.

Stochastic (14) — Like RSI but faster. Above 80 = overbought. Below 20 = oversold. Good for timing entries.

Williams %R — Above -20 = overbought. Below -80 = oversold. Confirms RSI and Stochastic readings.

OBV (On-Balance Volume) — Rising OBV with rising price = strong trend. Falling OBV with rising price = weak trend, potential reversal.

ATR — Average daily range of TSLA. Used to set realistic stop losses and targets. Higher ATR = more volatile, wider stops needed.

Implied Volatility — Market's expected TSLA movement. High IV = expensive options but bigger moves possible. Low IV = cheaper options, smaller expected moves.
